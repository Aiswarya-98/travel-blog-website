NATURAL BEAUTY

Kerala is renowned for its stunning natural landscapes, which include lush green hills, serene backwaters, palm-fringed beaches, and dense forests. The Western Ghats mountain range runs through the state, offering breathtaking views and opportunities for trekking and wildlife exploration.

BACKWATERS

Kerala's backwaters are a unique and iconic feature of the state. These intricate networks of canals, rivers, and lakes are best explored through houseboat cruises, providing a tranquil and immersive experience of rural life.

CULTURE AND TRADITION

Kerala has a rich cultural heritage with a blend of different art forms, classical dance, music, and festivals. Kathakali, Mohiniyattam, and Kalaripayattu are traditional art forms that have originated here.

BACKWATER PARADISE

ALAPPUZHA


Alappuzha or Alleppey is the administrative headquarters of Alappuzha district in state of Kerala, India. The Backwaters of Alappuzha are one of the most popular tourist attractions in India which attracts millions of domestic and international tourists.


KUTTANAD

Kuttanad is a region covering the Alappuzha, Kottayam and Pathanamthitta Districts, in the state of Kerala, India, well known for its vast paddy fields and geographical peculiarities. The region has the lowest altitude in India, and is one of the few places in the world where farming is carried on around 1.2 to 3.0 metres below sea level.

AMBALAPUZHA TEMPLE

Ambalappuzha Sree Krishna Swamy Temple is an Indian Hindu temple dedicated to Krishna at Ambalappuzha in Alappuzha district of Kerala. The temple is believed to have been built during 15th century AD

KAINAKARY

Kainakary is a shooting location for movie/TV soaps/music videos, etc. The village itself has numerous small water bodies, rivers, canals and ponds. People dwell near to canals and river banks mainly. Being part of Kuttanad, vast paddy lands make up the major part of the village. These paddy lands are below sea water level.


CAPITAL STATE

TRIVANDRUM


Thiruvananthapuram, formerly known as Trivandrum, is the capital of the Indian state of Kerala. It is the most populous city in Kerala with a population of 957,730 as of 2011.

Varkala is a major coastal municipality situated in the northern suburb of Trivandrum, capital city of the Indian state of Kerala.
